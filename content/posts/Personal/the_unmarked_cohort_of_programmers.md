A lot of things are uncertain in our world today; will we get wiped out by climate change in the next 50 years?, can we eventually colonize mars? or would we be able to make it there before a nuclear war breaks out?. Nevertheless, one thing is as certain as rain, programmers will eventually be referred to as pre-LLM and post-LLM. Just like my parents let me know about life before computers, so are the latter group of programmers going to drill into our heads the golden days of stackOverflow and google search.

However, I believe there's a class of programmers that are unaccounted for, and those are the ones who began learning right when LLM bots became mainstream(i.e. the release of ChatGPT in 2022). I have a bit of sympathy for them, because unfortunately for me, I am also one.

My story begins in 2022, right when I had just graduated high school. I applied to a free python developer bootcamp and got accepted. I was excited to learn my first programming language, and was even more excited about my potential capacity to transistion into fields like Machine learning and data science. Then 2 months into the bootcamp, ChatGPT hit the shelves, and I couldn't help but notice. I was in awe with my first usage. Yes I was only able to use it for about 30 minutes due to heavy server usage, but I had seen enough. This was going to take over industries, and maybe it could even help me in my programming journey. And help it did, every chance I got to use expend my limited usage, I took it.

Mind you at this point, I was the only one in my class who had knowlege about this technology. But since I am not a misely person, I began to introduce it to the others, in which I received some mixed reactions, where some embraced almost immediately, others more suspiciously, and some who didn't even want anything to do with it.

Since then, I have used LLMs as part of my programming tasks and I don't think it's going to change anytime soon. But what has changed over the years is my mindset towards programming. I realised that it being a programmer was much more than writing code, it required.....

From that, I dedicated more time to actually learningthe necessary syntax for any language I needed to learn, I educated myself on system designs, design patterns and more. This way I could actually use it more intectually, asking better questions. and using more as an ability booster than a dependency.

Hopefully, every other programmer in my "cohort" has reached this level of realization. I know you are out there, so stay strong.
