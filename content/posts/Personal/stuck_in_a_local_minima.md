For about a year now, I've felt this constant gnawing at my soul that I am stuck in a *local minimum*. 

It's a term that can be found in fields like [deep learning](https://en.wikipedia.org/wiki/Deep_learning) (it's actually implemented from calculus but we'll stick with the deep learning angle)

Here, during the training of a neural network model, the goal is to ensure that,  giving an input `x`, the difference between the prediction made by the model `y` and the actual value of the output is as low as possible. 

You can see this in tasks like classifying *dogs and cats* from images, predicting *house prices* from linear real estate data or generating *output text* giving an input text.

At the start of the training, the discrepancy between prediction and output is very high, but as the neural network keeps learning it get's better at understanding what the output should be for a given input, and by the end the discrepancy should be very low. This can be measured by a formula called the *loss function* (but we won't get into that for now).

Now if one is not careful to set the right hyperparameters (i.e. variables that can be tweaked to improve the model's prediction), after a particular point during the training the model will not be able to make progress in reducing the loss function. This point is called the local minimum.

You can think of the local minimum like a valley in a mountain range. The mountain range has several low points depending on what direction you are going. You may reach one of these points, but this does not mean you've reached the lowest absolute point (also known as *global minimum*)

And unless you're able to shift direction fast enough, you're stuck at that bottom of a valley unless you decide to climb back up to the peak of the mountain and choose another direction towards the global minimum (equivalent of restarting your model training with different hyperparameters like the *learning rate*)

And this is how I've felt every passing day, I am aware of my deficiencies so I strive to improve them or change them, but I still feel stuck.

Yes I've learned to curb distractions, build systems to make actions easier, focus on what priority work, act with urgency, struggle through the pain, track my activities and take risks.

Yet despite me feeling I'm pushing in the right direction, reality doesn't reflect that. No job, no money, no further education and nothing to show for what I know. Just me.

Yes, within this timeline, I've had bursts of success which before made me a bit happy but now I'm just numb to them. 

But very often, I hear or see, or realize or do something that makes me feel "this is the key to escaping my local minimum and finally finding progress in life". And just when I start working along that path, I am dashed down the slope and falling back into another local minimum.

Thankfully for me, I have hope. But just like tweaking your Adam Optimizer to 0.0003 doesn't actually mean your model is suddenly going become world class, so does hope not exactly correlate with reality. My hope keeps me moving towards the progress I long for, but my reality never reflects it. 

Everyday, I learn something new to change and to do, and I think "is this the key to moving forward? or is it just part of a new daily checklist I now have to be wary about?"

I'm writing this now as I sit down in my room with 10s of things I need to do. I believe if I do them, I will achieve progress, or will I?

Yes, yes I will. I have a responsibility to do right by my family, my friends, my God and most importantly myself.
